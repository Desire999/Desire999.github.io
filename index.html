Research Experience

Empathetic Reasoning in Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning Jun 2020 - Present
Research Assistant, supervised by Professor Weinan Zhang, SJTU

    We introduced a novel concept of empathy to symmetric Markov games to achieve explicit cooperation among agents, where each agent reasons the evaluation of other agents through its own model. Considering the mutual reasoning between agents, the impact of empathy could be recursive. Our proposed algorithm is compatible with common RL frameworks, like Q-Learning or Actor-Critic. To verify our mechanism of empathy-based explicit cooperation, we tested our algorithm in a delicately designed math game and battle in MAgent, where our algorithm can beat against popular baselines al least 70% times.

Camouflaged Physical Attack Jun 2020 - Sep 2020
Independent Research, supervised by Professor Yisen Wang, PKU

    We proposed an effective method to construct adversarial examples. Existing algorithms mainly focus on pixel-level optimization and ensure the generated images to be natural by carefully-designed objectives. This kind of algorithm does not pose any explicit constraints on the naturalness of generated samples and may lead to some strange images that will be quickly detected by humans. To overcome these shortcomings, We proposed manipulating high-level features, specifically the lightness and contrast. The carefully chosen high-level properties ensure that the modified images are still natural, and extensive experiments have verified our expectations.
